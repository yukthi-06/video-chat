# User Request: Maintain the original resolution and aspect ratio as the available source

## Goal
To record both local and remote video streams at their native source resolution and aspect ratio (e.g. 720p 16:9, or whatever resolution the camera/decoder outputs), avoiding squishing, stretching, or downsampling.

## Solution Details
1. **Dynamic Resolution Sensing**:
   - Removed the final static `width` and `height` properties from [WebRtcVideoRecorder.java](file:///x:/Projects_X/0_Active/8_Android_APK/04_Early_Stage/Video_Chat_APK_GITHUB/app/src/main/java/com/example/videochatapp/webrtc/WebRtcVideoRecorder.java).
   - Removed the hardcoded width/height parameters from the constructor of `WebRtcVideoRecorder` and its instantiation calls inside [CallActivity.java](file:///x:/Projects_X/0_Active/8_Android_APK/04_Early_Stage/Video_Chat_APK_GITHUB/app/src/main/java/com/example/videochatapp/activities/CallActivity.java).
2. **Deferred Codec Setup**:
   - Calling `start()` now only registers a `isStartRequested = true` intent.
   - When the first `VideoFrame` arrives inside `onFrame(VideoFrame frame)`:
     - The recorder queries the exact incoming frame dimensions using `frame.getRotatedWidth()` and `frame.getRotatedHeight()`.
     - The dimensions are aligned to multiples of 16 (required for efficient hardware H.264 encoding).
     - The internal `startInternal()` method is executed dynamically to configure `MediaCodec` and `MediaMuxer` at that exact target resolution and aspect ratio.
This ensures the output `.mp4` files maintain the original resolution and aspect ratio of the stream sources.
