# Walkthrough - Delayed Disconnect on Upload Completion

I have successfully updated the call teardown process to gracefully delay disconnection until all outgoing uploads and incoming downloads of the recorded video files have fully finished.

## Changes Made

### Video Recorder Finalization (`WebRtcVideoRecorder.java`)
- **Final Segment Callback**: Updated `stop()` in [WebRtcVideoRecorder.java](file:///x:/Projects_X/0_Active/8_Android_APK/04_Early_Stage/Video_Chat_APK_GITHUB/app/src/main/java/com/vypeensoft/videochatapp/webrtc/WebRtcVideoRecorder.java) to invoke the `onSegmentCompleted` listener when stopping. This guarantees that the final, partial segment (recorded between the last 60-second split and the time the call is hung up) is also processed and prepared for upload.

### Graceful Teardown Controller (`CallActivity.java`)
- **Upload Tracker**: Added `activeUploadsCount` (AtomicInteger) to [CallActivity.java](file:///x:/Projects_X/0_Active/8_Android_APK/04_Early_Stage/Video_Chat_APK_GITHUB/app/src/main/java/com/vypeensoft/videochatapp/activities/CallActivity.java).
- **Atomic Operations**: Increments `activeUploadsCount` when `sendVideoFile` begins a file stream, and decrements it inside the `finally` block on completion (or error).
- **Graceful Disconnect Loop**:
  - Rewrote `endCall()` to stop both local and remote recorders first (which forces the final local segment to split and queue up for upload).
  - Spawns a background thread that monitors both `activeUploadsCount` (outgoing files) and `activeReceivers.size()` (incoming files).
  - If transfers are still active, it displays a Toast message and sleeps for 1 second, repeating until the transfer counts hit zero.
  - Performs the actual WebSocket disconnect and finishes the activity only when all transfers are done.
- **Hooked Remote Disconnect**: Updated `onCallEnded()` to route through `endCall()` instead of immediately finishing, ensuring that a remote hangup doesn't clip our local uploads midway.
